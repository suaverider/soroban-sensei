Soroban Sensei
A focused 25-minute daily soroban routine for speed, accuracy, and mental visualization.

Built to feel like a complete practice session, not a loose pile of drills. One button starts a balanced routine that warms up your hands, sharpens bead recognition, trains mental soroban, and reinforces complements without guesswork.

Recommended Path Run the guided routine once per day, then use standalone drills only when you want extra repetitions.
What’s inside the daily routine:
01
Warm-Up (4 min)
Physical soroban warm-up. Two-digit mixed work under 100 with adaptive chain length.
02
Bead Reading (3 min)
Read bead patterns quickly and type the number they show. The guided routine can expand into three columns as your level rises.
03
Flash Anzan (10 min)
Short number flashes that train steady mental bead movement and retention.
04
Listening Anzan (5 min)
Numbers read aloud (yomiage) — add them in your head with no screen, the way a teacher calls them out in class.
05
Friends of 10 (2 min)
Fast complement recall for carries and borrows.
06
Friends of 5 (1 min)
Quick upper-bead complements for smoother technique.
No equipment needed for 4 of 5 phases. Bead Reading, Flash Anzan, Friends of 10, and Friends of 5 are fully on-screen. The warm-up works best with a physical soroban, but you can still train effectively without it.
What this is best for: consistent soroban skill training, stronger bead recognition, and more reliable mental visualization. It is designed as deliberate practice, not as a broad claim about general brain improvement. Individual results vary.
Beyond the guided routine: Standard Drill, Flash Anzan, Multiplication, and Complement Sprint are also available as standalone modes whenever you want targeted extra work.
❓ How do I use this?
  1. Click Start Training below to enter the app.
  2. Click the gold Daily Course (25 min) button — that runs the full routine and is all you need.
  3. Follow the phases: an optional warm-up with a physical soroban, then bead reading, anzan, listening anzan, and complements.
  4. Each phase has a short screen explaining what to do — just hit Continue.
  5. The program adapts to you — get answers right and it gets harder; struggle and it backs off.
  6. On a phone or tablet? It works right here in your browser. To keep it handy, use your browser menu’s “Add to Home Screen” for an app-like icon — no app store needed.
  7. Do this every day. That’s it — consistency is everything.
❓ Do I need a physical soroban?
  1. For the warm-up phase, yes — use your physical soroban to work the problems
  2. For bead reading, anzan, and complements, no physical soroban needed — it’s all on screen and in your head
  3. If you don’t have a physical soroban yet, tap Skip Phase during the warm-up to jump straight to the on-screen drills — you can do everything else without one.
❓ How does progress saving work?
  1. Your progress saves automatically in this browser, on this device.
  2. Use the same browser on the same device to keep your progress — a different browser or device starts fresh.
  3. Clearing your browser data will also delete saved progress.
  4. Use the Export button in Practice History to download a backup file, and Import to restore it anytime.
  5. We recommend exporting a backup every week or so.

Visualization Tip
Eyes open. Look at the number on screen. As you see it, picture your soroban’s beads moving — don’t try to “see” a perfect crystal-clear abacus image. At first it’s fuzzy and that’s fine. Focus on the movement: “push 3 earth beads up.” Some teachers recommend moving your thumb and index finger slightly in the air as if touching real beads — this bridges the physical and mental. With practice, the image gets clearer and the finger movements fade away. You’re building a new brain pathway — it takes weeks, not days.

Recommended Daily Routine (25 min) — use the Guided Routine button above to auto-run this





4 min

Standard Drill Warm-Up
Intermediate · 2-digit · Stay under 100 · Mixed ops. Physical soroban work — keeps your fingers sharp. Four minutes gives you ~12 problems with the adaptive chain length getting harder as your accuracy climbs.

3 min

Bead Reading
The guided routine starts with two-column bead patterns and can move to three columns as your guided anzan level rises. This builds instant bead-to-number recognition without staying artificially capped at double digits.

10 min

Anzan (mental visualization)
Numbers flash on screen — NO beads, NO answers. You picture the soroban in your mind. Starts at Level 4 (3 numbers, ones rod). If you pass 2 sets in a row, it advances. If you fail 1 set, it drops back immediately. Auto-adjusts to stay at your edge.

5 min

Listening Anzan (audio / yomiage)
Numbers are read aloud — no screen. Hold the running total in your head, the way a teacher calls them out in a Japanese classroom. Starts a little easier than the visual round and adapts up or down with you. Needs your volume on.

2 min

Friends of 10
Speed drill: a number appears, you click what + that number = 10. Builds automatic complement recall for carries and borrows. ~80 reps at fast pace.

1 min

Friends of 5
Speed drill: a number appears, you click what + that number = 5. Essential for upper bead operations. ~40 reps. Each complement type gets its own phase with a transition screen between them.





Why a structured routine matters: Most soroban practice is unstructured — you sit down and do whatever feels right. This program keeps your daily work consistent: warm up your hands, sharpen bead recognition, train mental visualization, and drill complements in one fixed session. The difficulty adjusts so you stay challenged without guessing what to practice next.
